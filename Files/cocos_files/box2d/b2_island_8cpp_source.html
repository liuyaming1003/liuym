00028 /*
00029 Position Correction Notes
00030 =========================
00031 I tried the several algorithms for position correction of the 2D revolute joint.
00032 I looked at these systems:
00033 - simple pendulum (1m diameter sphere on massless 5m stick) with initial angular velocity of 100 rad/s.
00034 - suspension bridge with 30 1m long planks of length 1m.
00035 - multi-link chain with 30 1m long links.
00036 
00037 Here are the algorithms:
00038 
00039 Baumgarte - A fraction of the position error is added to the velocity error. There is no
00040 separate position solver.
00041 
00042 Pseudo Velocities - After the velocity solver and position integration,
00043 the position error, Jacobian, and effective mass are recomputed. Then
00044 the velocity constraints are solved with pseudo velocities and a fraction
00045 of the position error is added to the pseudo velocity error. The pseudo
00046 velocities are initialized to zero and there is no warm-starting. After
00047 the position solver, the pseudo velocities are added to the positions.
00048 This is also called the First Order World method or the Position LCP method.
00049 
00050 Modified Nonlinear Gauss-Seidel (NGS) - Like Pseudo Velocities except the
00051 position error is re-computed for each constraint and the positions are updated
00052 after the constraint is solved. The radius vectors (aka Jacobians) are
00053 re-computed too (otherwise the algorithm has horrible instability). The pseudo
00054 velocity states are not needed because they are effectively zero at the beginning
00055 of each iteration. Since we have the current position error, we allow the
00056 iterations to terminate early if the error becomes smaller than b2_linearSlop.
00057 
00058 Full NGS or just NGS - Like Modified NGS except the effective mass are re-computed
00059 each time a constraint is solved.
00060 
00061 Here are the results:
00062 Baumgarte - this is the cheapest algorithm but it has some stability problems,
00063 especially with the bridge. The chain links separate easily close to the root
00064 and they jitter as they struggle to pull together. This is one of the most common
00065 methods in the field. The big drawback is that the position correction artificially
00066 affects the momentum, thus leading to instabilities and false bounce. I used a
00067 bias factor of 0.2. A larger bias factor makes the bridge less stable, a smaller
00068 factor makes joints and contacts more spongy.
00069 
00070 Pseudo Velocities - the is more stable than the Baumgarte method. The bridge is
00071 stable. However, joints still separate with large angular velocities. Drag the
00072 simple pendulum in a circle quickly and the joint will separate. The chain separates
00073 easily and does not recover. I used a bias factor of 0.2. A larger value lead to
00074 the bridge collapsing when a heavy cube drops on it.
00075 
00076 Modified NGS - this algorithm is better in some ways than Baumgarte and Pseudo
00077 Velocities, but in other ways it is worse. The bridge and chain are much more
00078 stable, but the simple pendulum goes unstable at high angular velocities.
00079 
00080 Full NGS - stable in all tests. The joints display good stiffness. The bridge
00081 still sags, but this is better than infinite forces.
00082 
00083 Recommendations
00084 Pseudo Velocities are not really worthwhile because the bridge and chain cannot
00085 recover from joint separation. In other cases the benefit over Baumgarte is small.
00086 
00087 Modified NGS is not a robust method for the revolute joint due to the violent
00088 instability seen in the simple pendulum. Perhaps it is viable with other constraint
00089 types, especially scalar constraints where the effective mass is a scalar.
00090 
00091 This leaves Baumgarte and Full NGS. Baumgarte has small, but manageable instabilities
00092 and is very fast. I don't think we can escape Baumgarte, especially in highly
00093 demanding cases where high constraint fidelity is not needed.
00094 
00095 Full NGS is robust and easy on the eyes. I recommend this as an option for
00096 higher fidelity simulation and certainly for suspension bridges and long chains.
00097 Full NGS might be a good choice for ragdolls, especially motorized ragdolls where
00098 joint separation can be problematic. The number of NGS iterations can be reduced
00099 for better performance without harming robustness much.
00100 
00101 Each joint in a can be handled differently in the position solver. So I recommend
00102 a system where the user can select the algorithm on a per joint basis. I would
00103 probably default to the slower Full NGS and let the user select the faster
00104 Baumgarte method in performance critical scenarios.
00105 */

00107 /*
00108 Cache Performance
00109 
00110 The Box2D solvers are dominated by cache misses. Data structures are designed
00111 to increase the number of cache hits. Much of misses are due to random access
00112 to body data. The constraint structures are iterated over linearly, which leads
00113 to few cache misses.
00114 
00115 The bodies are not accessed during iteration. Instead read only data, such as
00116 the mass values are stored with the constraints. The mutable data are the constraint
00117 impulses and the bodies velocities/positions. The impulses are held inside the
00118 constraint structures. The body velocities/positions are held in compact, temporary
00119 arrays to increase the number of cache hits. Linear and angular velocity are
00120 stored in a single array since multiple arrays lead to multiple misses.
00121 */

00127     [sin(theta) cos(theta) ]

00130 
00131 Let q1 = cos(theta), q2 = sin(theta).
00132 R = [q1 -q2]
00133     [q2  q1]
